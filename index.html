About me

I'm Aga and I like...

I’m a girl who studied literature and now is discovering poetry in coding. My new found love for tech came about while traveling around New Zealand in the middle of Covid-19 pandemic. It’s where - stranded for weeks - I had a lot of time to think about my life goals and to rediscover my everlasting interest in technology. I’ve always wanted to learn how to blend the art of UI design with skill of programming to deliver an immersive and engaging user experience. Also, seeing my family and friends who work in IT building impactful & intelligent solutions helped me to decide - I want to be a Web Developer, want to be at the epicentre of the current digital revolution!

After some research and a few internet courses, I finally grew in confidence and enrolled onto Just IT’s Developers Programme. It has given me a broad exposure to coding languages and frameworks which allowed me to create solutions for a wide variety of projects. I also believe that my diverse educational background - diplomas in Literature, Journalism, Social economy and Coaching - can be an asset and give another dimension to my newly gained technical skills. Same applies to my work experience. Working as a project manager for NGOs and then assistant manager for a coffee shop in London I learnt how to solve complex problems. To creatively think about unorthodox approaches. To be apaptable and proactive. I now look forward to implement these skills and grow in the industry as a Front End Developer in my next role.

Friends Quiz

Javascript App

A Simple quiz app built using HTML, CSS and Vanilla JS, that utilises local storage and enables users to save their highest scores. Layout was designed to be simple, clear, easy to read and navigate. Questions are generated randomly to enhance users' experience and keep some level of surprise the second time they play. Once the question is answered, the app indicates if the user’s choice was correct or wrong (colour indication) and automatically navigates to another question. The progress bar on the top indicates the number of remaining questions and the score field in the other corner is updated every time the user gets the answer right. Once the quiz is finished it gives users a feedback and an options either to save their scores, play again or go to the starting page where they can access the leaderboard by clicking ‘High scores’ button.

News Assistant

A Voice Recognition React News Application

In this project I relied primarily on Alan AI - a revolutionary yet easy to integrtate software that allows to add voice capability to many apps. News Assistant uses external API (News API) to display news by searched terms, sources and categories. A user can use their voice to give commands - such as asking Alan to display the recent news, to read headlines or open the particular article. Thanks to adding a bulid-in script it can also provide weather information. The interface was build using React and Material-UI framework.
